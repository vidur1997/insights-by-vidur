Market Entry Strategy | Blackmont Consulting#

Engagement: Strategic analysis for market entry in a GBP 400M+ sector

Challenge: Client needed to evaluate market opportunity, competitive landscape, and optimal entry strategy for a fragmented B2B market with multiple incumbent players.

Approach:

  1. Market Sizing — Built bottom-up and top-down models to validate market opportunity and identify growth trajectories through 2030

  2. Competitive Intelligence — Analysed 4 major players across 8 dimensions including pricing, distribution, product range, and market positioning

  3. Customer Segmentation — Developed targeting framework based on customer value potential and acquisition feasibility

  4. Financial Modelling — Projected Year 1 revenue scenarios with sensitivity analysis on key assumptions

Deliverables:

  • Executive presentation to client leadership
  • Strategic roadmap with 8 prioritised recommendations
  • Go-to-market playbook with phased implementation plan

Impact:

Academic Research#

Geographic Concentration in Global Semiconductor Manufacturing#

MSc Dissertation | University of Exeter | 2025-2026

Research Question: Why have USD 95B in government subsidies failed to reduce geographic concentration in semiconductor manufacturing?

Methodology:

  1. Quantitative Analysis — Trade flow data, production capacity metrics, and market share trends (2015-2024)

  2. Total Cost of Ownership Model — Comparative analysis of manufacturing economics across US, Taiwan, and emerging regions

  3. Ecosystem Mapping — Assessment of talent, supplier networks, and infrastructure dependencies

Key Findings:

METRIC                          VALUE
------                          -----
Global Market Size              USD 500B+
Taiwan Market Share             31%
US Manufacturing Cost Premium   +56% vs Taiwan
Government Subsidies (Global)   USD 95B+
Concentration Trend             Increasing despite subsidies

Strategic Implications:

  • Subsidies alone cannot replicate ecosystem advantages (talent density, supplier proximity, infrastructure maturity)
  • Geographic diversification requires 10-15 year horizon and sustained investment
  • Supply chain risk remains elevated for advanced node manufacturing

Skills Applied: Python, SQL, Data Visualisation, Economic Analysis, Strategic Research
